Overview

Garden On Canvas is inspired by the atmosphere of a wild garden in late spring. The website combines the 3D models with painted abstract backgrounds to create a soft, exhibition-like experience. The overall design is intentionally minimal so that the models remain the focus of each scene.

Development



Bee
I followed the tutorial Bee Animation by PIXXO 3D to create my bee model. One of the main development challenges involved the wing animation. Initially, a mirror modifier had been used during modelling, which later caused issues when animating the wings independently. This was resolved by replacing the mirrored wings with separate individual wings so they could move correctly.
Blender view


Flower
The flower model was developed using a tutorial by Atti. This was the most detailed model to create due to the layered petals, and particle system. The original Blender model included mixed shaders and particle systems for details such as fine hairs and feathery petal textures. The particles were converted to mesh to transfer correctly to the website.
Blender view


Water Pump
The water pump model was created using a tutorial by CG VOICE. Initially, physics simulations were used in Blender to create flowing water. Although the simulation worked inside Blender, it could not be exported successfully into the GLB file. Instead, the water effect was recreated using an animated curve mesh. Separate moving parts were also required so the pump handle could animate independently.

Design Choices

Bee
Initially I used a mirror modifier to ease building of the model. However this caused problems during animation. Mirrored components like wings were deleted to add animation to the model. The model animations include movement of the bee in a stationery position, and flight.

Flower
The flower included a particle system which could not be exported in GLB format. This was changed to mesh to maintain the visuals of the flower. The model animation includes rotating the petals around each other, and releasing of pollen which was added using JavaScript.

Water Pump
The water pump was modelled leaving the handle area separate to facilitate motion during animation. The model animation includes movement of the handle, together with water pouring out of the pump. The water was simulated using a curve and droplets using a mesh object.

Accessibility

Accessibility was considered through readable fonts, clearly labelled buttons, visible hover, and text descriptions for each 3D model. The website also includes multiple camera angles, rotation controls, and adjustable lighting so users can view the models more clearly from different positions.

Alternative text was added to images where possible, and the layout was tested across different screen sizes to improve readability and usability.

Testing

Bee
The bee model required repeated testing to improve the animation and lighting. During development, the lighting often became too strong from certain camera angles, causing parts of the model to appear washed out. Different light positions and intensities were tested to create softer shadows and more balanced highlights. The problem caused by the mirror modifier was solved during the testing lab.

Flower
The flower model required extensive testing due to export issues between Blender and Three.js. The particle systems and mix shaders that appeared correctly in Blender disappeared when exported as a GLB file. Alternative approaches were tested, including converting particles into meshes and replacing textures with a UV image.

Water Pump
The water pump required testing of both animation and interaction. Problems occurred when toggling the wireframe mode because different moving parts of the model reacted inconsistently. The water animation was also adjusted multiple times to ensure the movement appeared natural.

Generative AI

Generative AI was used for support during development and debugging. It helped identify possible solutions for converting Blender particle systems into mesh or curve-based alternatives for the flower model.

It was also used to troubleshoot issues with exporting animated models, model orientation, JavaScript errors, canvas resizing, and Three.js interaction features such as toggling the wireframe. The final design and implementation decisions were reviewed and adjusted manually.
